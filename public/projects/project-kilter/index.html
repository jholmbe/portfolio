project: kilter

minimalist spreadsheet application for fitness
Repository
project: kilter is a design project for UMD's INST451 class. The professor requested the class to design for a personal passion; my focus was weightlifting. Also, teaching staff provided mentorship during our five design sprints in the semester. The objective of the class was UI/UX and not the programming of the interfaces.

Weightlifters with 2+ years experience lack tools truly optimized for their workflow. Spreadsheets and plain-text methods are functional and customizable, but weren't designed with weightlifters in mind. project: kilter seeks to optimize current methods of tracking personal fitness by offering a user-friendly interface, modular features, and leveraging the community through the plugins system.

FINAL UI

This was an independent project. I developed a lightweight spreadsheet application with the guidance of real users. The product niche is the modularity of the plugins system, enabling lifters to choose their workflow. In combination, there is cross-platform compatibility (desktop & mobile), 3 included togglable plugins, and developer support. The minimalist design is inspired by my desire to declutter modern fitness interfaces, and I like the color green.

DESIGN PROCESS

This was an independent project. Design sprint 1 was for research while the rest was focused on increasing the fidelity of the prototypes. My approach to design was very top-down—having decided on specific features and figuring out how to add it. My specific process looked like analyzing competitors features or bits of software that have served me in the past and creating my own variation of it. I used sites like Dribble and explored many different products (not just fitness ones) to inspire my design.

USER & MARKET RESEARCH

Throughout the project, I had reoccuring interviews with 5 intermediate-advanced weight lifters. In the initial phase I had them all answer a google survey I put together. I made conclusions on primary motivations, important values, and adding specific features, like an AI chatbot. The data from the discussions allowed me to create a user persona, journey maps, compare competitors in the current market.
